About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at Missouri University of Science and Technology, where I work on cutting-edge projects such as acid mist suppression in copper electrowinning, battery fire safety, and air filtration using nanofibers. My expertise lies in nanofiber-based air/water filtration, energy storage, electrochemical safety, and industrial pollution mitigation, leveraging advanced manufacturing techniques like electrospinning and solution blowing.

My research contributes to environmental sustainability through interdisciplinary innovation across nanotechnology, materials science, and engineering. With over 7 years of experience, I focus on scalable and cost-effective solutions with real-world impact.
